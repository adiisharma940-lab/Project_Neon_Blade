Heritage // Legacy

Born to Dominate

The Nissan GT-R is not merely a car — it is a philosophy forged in steel, rubber, and relentless pursuit of perfection. Rooted in the legendary Skyline lineage that terrorized the 1990s racing circuits, the R35 generation arrived in 2007 and immediately redefined what a production car could achieve. It embarrassed Ferraris and Porsches at half their price. It was an act of defiance — Japanese engineering declaring war on European tradition.

The 2013 Black Edition represents the pinnacle of this evolution: a factory-tuned, performance-optimized variant that wears its intentions on every sculpted surface. Dark chrome accents, blacked-out Rays forged wheels, and exclusive interior stitching signal this isn't the standard GT-R — it's the sharper edge of an already razor-sharp machine.

Powertrain // VR38DETT

The Heart of a God

Beneath the long aluminium hood sits the VR38DETT — a hand-assembled 3.8-litre twin-turbocharged V6 that Nissan's master engine builder Shoichi Miyatani signs by hand on every single unit. Not a robot. Not an assembly line. A human being accountable for every tolerance, every torque spec, every seal.

Twin Garrett turbochargers spool with ferocious urgency, delivering linear, relentless thrust from idle to redline. The dry-sump oil system means it corners without oil starvation. The engine block is aluminium. The cylinder liners are cast iron. Every component exists to serve one purpose: transfer energy to the road with zero compromise.

Transmission // ATTESA E-TS

Lightning-Fast Shifts

The GT-R's 6-speed dual-clutch transaxle (GR6) is mounted at the rear axle, creating near-perfect 53/47 weight distribution. Shifts happen in 0.15 seconds — faster than a human nerve impulse. Three driving modes — Normal, Sport, and R — adjust shift speed, aggression, and clutch engagement to match the moment.

The ATTESA E-TS AWD system monitors g-forces, steering angle, and wheel slip hundreds of times per second. Under hard acceleration, it can push up to 100% of torque to the rear. In a corner gone wrong, it redistributes instantly. It doesn't just prevent mistakes — it makes the impossible routine.

Aerodynamics // Chassis

Sculpted by Wind

Every line on the GT-R's body is purposeful. The front fascia channels air beneath the car to the underbody diffuser, generating downforce at speed without drag-inducing wings. The rear ducktail spoiler presses the rear axle into the road precisely as grip is needed. Wide rear haunches house 285mm tyres that claw into tarmac.

The chassis is a rigid steel monocoque with front and rear multi-link suspension tuned by Bilstein, adjustable via the DampTronic system across three modes. Brembo brakes with six-piston front calipers haul the GT-R from 100 to 0 in just 31 metres.

Black Edition // Exclusives

Beyond Standard

The Black Edition isn't a trim package. It's a statement. Exclusive 20-inch Rays forged aluminium wheels in Midnight Opal finish are lighter than cast wheels by 2 kg per corner, directly reducing unsprung mass for faster steering response. Carbon fibre interior trim extends to door inserts and centre console.

Interior // Technology

The Cockpit Mentality

Step inside and the world outside ceases to exist. The driver-focused cockpit wraps around you with purpose and intent. Every control is within reach. The multi-function 7-inch display can be configured to show turbo boost, g-forces, lap times, tyre pressures, and exhaust sound profiles simultaneously.

The 11-speaker Bose Premium Audio delivers 710 watts of concert-hall clarity. Dual-zone climate control maintains perfect conditions. And despite its fearsome performance credentials, the GT-R offers legitimate rear seats and a 244-litre boot — a machine with principles and practicality in equal measure.

Culture // Godzilla

Godzilla Lives

The press nicknamed it Godzilla — a monster from Japan capable of crushing anything in its path. The name stuck. In Gran Turismo, it was the car every player wanted. On the Nürburgring, it held production car lap records. It made supercar ownership seem irresponsible, when a GT-R could match a $200,000 Italian exotic for $80,000.

The 2013 Black Edition is one of the most celebrated variants of this legend. These cars are already appreciating. In two decades, they will be icons — artefacts of a golden era when engineers prioritised the purity of driving above all else.
