Heritage // Legacy

Born to Dominate

The Nissan GT-R is not merely a car — it is a philosophy forged in steel, rubber, and relentless pursuit of perfection. Rooted in the legendary Skyline lineage that terrorized 1990s circuits, the R35 generation arrived in 2007 and redefined what a production car could achieve. It embarrassed Ferraris and Porsches at half their price.

The 2013 Black Edition represents the pinnacle of this evolution: factory-tuned, performance-optimized, wearing its intentions on every sculpted surface. Dark chrome accents, blacked-out Rays forged wheels, exclusive interior stitching — this is the sharper edge of an already razor-sharp machine.

Powertrain // VR38DETT

Heart of a God

Beneath the aluminium hood sits the VR38DETT — a hand-assembled 3.8-litre twin-turbocharged V6 signed by master builder Shoichi Miyatani. Not a robot. A human accountable for every tolerance, every seal.

Twin Garrett turbochargers spool with ferocious urgency, linear and relentless from idle to redline. The dry-sump system corners without oil starvation. Every component exists to transfer energy with zero compromise.

Aerodynamics // Chassis

Sculpted by Wind

Every line on the GT-R's body is purposeful. The front fascia channels air beneath the car to the underbody diffuser, generating downforce without drag. Wide rear haunches house 285mm tyres clawing into tarmac.

Bilstein DampTronic suspension across three modes. Brembo six-piston front calipers haul the GT-R from 100 to 0 in just 31 metres.
